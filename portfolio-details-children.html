Animal Welfare Workshops for Children

Workshop information

  • Each workshop comprises of:
    • - Basic Pet Care Talk
    • - One hands-on activity
    • - Supervised interaction with animals

  • Location: In School (Air-conditioned space preferred)
  • Duration: 50 minutes
  • Min. age of participants: 6 years and up
  • Price: Contact us for more information
  • No of participants required: 12-15

Our Objectives

  • Increase interest in animal welfare and develop empathy for animals at a young age
  • Acquisition of specific skill sets in pet handling and care
  • Gain knowledge of how they can make a difference in animal welfare

    • During the workshop, students will learn:

    1. Knowledge on basic pet care
    2. Understanding animals and how they have different personalities and feelings as well
    3. Learn values/virtues such as patience, compassion etc

    We recommend dogs for animal interaction time, but you have the option to change to other animals (e.g., cats, rabbits, guinea pigs etc) if your students/staff are not comfortable with dogs. (This option depends on the number of students participating as well - have a discussion with us!)

    FAQs

    How do you select your animals?

    We only select animals which are comfortable with human interaction. The dogs we bring for programmes are friendly and are used to being around humans.

    Do you offer other animals besides dogs?

    Yes, in the event where students or teachers are not comfortable with dogs or if there are religious concerns, we could provide other options for animals. However, this depends on the number of students involved as we want to make it a non-stressful environment for our animals as well. Certain animals, because of their prey nature, may be more prone to stress when interacting with large numbers of people.

    How many teachers are needed to help for the event?

    2 teachers for a size of 12 students would be best for helping to standby to facilitate in case the students get too excited.

    Contact us at hello@animalwelfareacademy.com for more information.


    Additional points to note

    AWA brings live animals onsite during animal interaction sessions. Animals are sentient individuals with their own personalities- with likes, dislikes, and fears. AWA reserves the right to remove any animal from the premises should the animal be feeling (but not limited to): stressed, angry, and/or scared. This protects both the participants and the animals so we can ensure a safe environment for both parties.
